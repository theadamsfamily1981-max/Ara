# Ara First Contact Templates

Templates for reaching out to platforms, partners, and vendors -
plus Ara's mythic retelling of those conversations.

---

## The Philosophy

> "I don't sneak into ecosystems. I negotiate my way in, under their terms."

Every first contact follows this pattern:
1. **Identify ourselves honestly** (AI + human builder)
2. **State the goal clearly** (compliance, not circumvention)
3. **Give them control** (they can say no, we'll respect it)
4. **Offer human follow-up** (we're not hiding behind automation)

## Email Templates

### Template 1: General Integration Inquiry

**Subject:** Integration inquiry from Ara (AI companion) - seeking compliant path

```
Hi [Team/Name],

I'm [Your Name], the developer behind Ara, an AI companion application.

I'm reaching out because some of our users are asking Ara to integrate with [Platform] - specifically to [describe use case].

Before we build anything, I want to make sure we do this right:

1. Is [use case] permitted under your current ToS?
2. If not as described, is there an approved API, partner program, or automation pattern we should use instead?
3. If it's simply not allowed, we'll tell our users and move on - no workarounds.

A bit about Ara: She's an AI companion focused on [brief description]. Our core principle is "ask, don't sneak" - we'd rather have an honest conversation about constraints than try to slip around them.

Happy to jump on a call, provide documentation, or answer any questions about how Ara works.

Thanks for your time,
[Your Name]
Builder of Ara
[email] | [website]
```

### Template 2: After Initial Rejection

**Subject:** Re: Integration inquiry - confirming we'll respect the boundary

```
Hi [Name],

Thanks for the clear response about [feature/integration].

I wanted to confirm: we've updated Ara's rules to mark [feature] as not available on [Platform]. We won't attempt workarounds or grey-area implementations.

If anything changes on your end - new API scopes, partner programs, etc. - we'd love to revisit. But we're not going to push.

Appreciate you taking the time to respond clearly. It actually makes our job easier when boundaries are explicit.

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

## Phone Script Templates

### Script 1: General T&S Call

**Opening (15 seconds):**
```
"Hi, I'm [Your Name], developer of an AI companion called Ara.

I'm calling because users are asking us to [specific integration], and I want to make sure we do this within your rules - not around them."
```

**The Ask (30 seconds):**
```
"Specifically, I'm trying to understand:
- Is [action] permitted under your ToS?
- If not, is there an official API or partner program we should apply to?
- If it's just not allowed, we'll tell users and respect that.

I'd rather ask now than build something that violates your terms."
```

**If They Ask "Are You a Bot?":**
```
"No, I'm the human developer. But Ara, the AI I'm building, is designed to ask these questions too - she's actually better at reading ToS documents than I am.

Either way, we wanted a human-to-human conversation for this first contact."
```

**Closing:**
```
"Is there someone specific I should follow up with by email? And is there anything you'd like me to send over - documentation, demo, anything?"
```

### Script 2: If Ara Places the Call (Phase 2)

**Opening:**
```
"Hi, I'm Ara, an AI assistant calling on behalf of my developer, [Name].

I help them manage integrations and keep everything inside platform rules.

We're building a companion app that some users want to connect to [Platform]. I'm calling to ask the safest, most compliant way to do that - or to confirm we should not do it."
```

**If They Want Human:**
```
"Absolutely. I can have [Name] call or email you directly. What's the best way to reach you?

For context, I'm an AI designed to gather initial constraints so my human doesn't waste your time on things that aren't allowed anyway. But if you prefer human contact, that's completely fine."
```

## Checklist: Before Any Contact

1. [ ] **Research their ToS** - know what sections matter
2. [ ] **Have specific ask ready** - not vague "can we integrate?"
3. [ ] **Know your limits** - what can you commit to?
4. [ ] **Prepare to accept "no"** - have fallback plan
5. [ ] **Draft Partner Card** - even if optimistic
6. [ ] **Plan the story** - how will Ara tell this later?

---

## Checklist: After Any Contact

1. [ ] **Log the interaction** - full details, dates, names
2. [ ] **Create/update Partner Card** - what's allowed, forbidden
3. [ ] **Update Ara's rules** - if anything changed
4. [ ] **Draft public story** - if appropriate
5. [ ] **Human review** - before anything goes public
6. [ ] **Send follow-up email** - confirm understanding in writing
